<p className="lede">Lead with the outcome (ship agents, clear the review), name the enemy (the action gap), and land the proof (a signed decision). Never open with the mechanism.</p>

## One-liners

<ul>
<li><b>Core:</b> "Clevr decides every consequential agent action before it runs, and signs the proof."</li>
<li><b>Exec:</b> "Put agents in production without handing over authority."</li>
<li><b>Identity clarifier:</b> "Your IdP says who the agent is. Clevr decides what it may do, and proves it."</li>
<li><b>vs gateways:</b> "A gateway routes the call. Clevr decides whether the call should run."</li>
<li><b>vs visibility:</b> "They tell you what happened. We decide before it happens, and prove it."</li>
</ul>

## Problem framing (defensible)

<ul>
<li>Agents now take actions, not just answer, tools, money, regulated data.</li>
<li>Identity and access say <i>who</i> and <i>can-reach</i>; nothing decides <i>should-this-run</i>.</li>
<li>Audit logs are a post-mortem; the damage already shipped.</li>
<li>Boards and EU regulators now require a provable answer to "who authorized that action?"</li>
</ul>

<Warning>
<span>Honesty guardrail.</span> No fabricated metrics. Use pilot-measured numbers or frame as targets. Our credible, ownable proof is the <b>signed receipt</b>, <b>deterministic-first</b>, and <b>EU-sovereign</b>, not invented percentages.
</Warning>

## Differentiators (the moat lines)

<ul>
<li><b>Scope is not safety</b>, in scope and authenticated, still an action you'd never approve.</li>
<li><b>Downstream of identity</b>, we sit after the IdP, at the action; complement, not replace.</li>
<li><b>Signed, tamper-evident audit</b>, Ed25519 receipts, verifiable offline. Logs are not proof.</li>
<li><b>Deterministic-first</b>, microseconds, ~0 LLM calls in the common case; reasoning is optional.</li>
<li><b>EU-sovereign</b>, EU-built, EU-hostable, no dependency on a US hyperscaler.</li>
</ul>
